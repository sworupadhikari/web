ANHEDONIA

The lack of detail in the background is unnerving. The dark and muddy colours of green, brown and black with a blurry texture does not give away much. We do not know who this is. He wears a brown tunic that looks like its been worn too much. He looks skinny but these physical descriptors raise more questions than they answer. What does he do? What is he thinking? What is he looking at? Why does he have a stool in the middle of the room? The painting provides nothing. This, however, means that we can provide answers for ourselves.

Rādhā Kṛṣṇa – The Glance

Love, as many philosophers put it, lies in a realm beyond the mundane. Language itself, which has evolved over thousands of years, fails at describing love. Love makes us, at the same time, the most vulnerable and the most resilient. Beethoven, the famous composer, in a letter to his “immortal beloved” writes, ‘at once your love makes me the happiest and unhappiest of mortals. Little did Beethoven know; this would apply not only to mortals but to the divine also. The scene depicted in Roshan Dangol’s Rādhā Kṛṣṇa shows this very idea.

KALACAKRA

Tantra is a religious and philosophical movement that is thought to have begun in India soon after the beginning of the common era. Literally meaning “to weave” or to “extend” the practice involves the usage of rituals, mantra, meditative practices and yogic theory to reach subtle levels of mind such that enlightenment can be achieved most efficiently. Both Hinduism and Buddhism have developed an expansive literature around tantric practice, each with its own distinct features and characteristics.
